Contributions and Expenses to the EU budget

Since the foundation of the European Union, its aim has always been to create a peaceful, efficient and economically stable unity of countries. To achieve this higher purpose a joint budget was set up and each EU state pays its contribution to this account. This huge piggy pank is then smashed and redistributed aiming for the best progress of the continent as a whole. We aim to visualise the absolute contributions and expenses to the EU budget and intent to analyse in what ways the redistribution of state money makes the EU flourish.

Specification of EU income

In 2019, the European Union found €174.305.600.000 on its bank account. There are 4 main categories in which this revenue was collected from its member states.

  • Value Added Tax (VAT)-Based Own Resource
    VAT is a harmonised tax rate based on the total taxes a country collects. It is capped at 50% of a country's Gross National Income (GNI).
  • Gross National Income (GNI)-Based Own Resource
    An additional resource based on the GNI of a country. It ensures that the general budget of the Union is always initially balanced.
  • Traditional Own Resources
    Consists of customs duties on imports from outside the EU. Each individual country pays this directly to the EU. In the past there was also a sugar production quoate system, but this has come to an end in 2017.
  • Other Revenue
    Various items such as taxes on EU workers' salaries, interest rates on late payments and fines and contributions of non-EU countries to research programs.

Expenditure of EU income

In 2019, the European Union assigned €159.095.500.000 to its member states. There are 6 main categories in which this money is invested per country.

  • Smart and Inclusive Growth
    Developing the economy based on knowledge and innovation. This economy aims for high employment delivering social and territorial cohesion.
  • Sustainable Growth: Natural Resources
    Promoting a more resource-efficient, greener and more competitive economy.
  • Security and Citizenship
    Focussing on security and rights of its citizens. Ranges from migration and border to control to food safety programs.
  • Global Europe
    Putting the EU in world's perspective. It covers all foreign policy, including international development and humanitarian aid.
  • Administration
    All expenditures to EU institutions and its staff.
  • Special Instruments
    Funding for unforeseen events for example natural disasters or major world trade patterns that displace workers.
